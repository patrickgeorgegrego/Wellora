DISEASES


- Cancer
   "Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body. These abnormal cells divide and grow at a rapid pace, forming a mass or tumor, which can interfere with normal bodily functions. If the growth is not controlled, it can spread to other parts of the body through the bloodstream or lymphatic system in a process called metastasis."

Causes:
   Causes: Cancer can be caused by genetic mutations, lifestyle factors (like smoking, excessive sun exposure, or diet), environmental exposures, and certain infections.

Symptoms:
   Symptoms of cancer can vary widely but often include unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, pain, changes in skin, or lumps that can be felt under the skin.

-Heart diseases
   Heart diseases (also known as cardiovascular diseases, or CVDs) refer to a group of disorders affecting the heart and blood vessels. These diseases are among the leading causes of death worldwide. Heart diseases can affect the heart's muscles, valves, electrical system, or blood supply.

Causes:
   High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)
High Cholesterol
Smoking
Diabetes
Obesity
Sedentary Lifestyle
Unhealthy Diet
Excessive Alcohol Consumption
Family History (Genetics)

Symptoms:
   The symptoms of heart disease vary depending on the type, but common signs include:

Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
Shortness of breath
Fatigue
Palpitations or irregular heartbeat
Swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet
Dizziness or fainting

-Diabetes
   "Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that occurs when the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) levels. This can happen due to either insufficient insulin production or the body's cells becoming resistant to insulin's effects. Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that helps cells absorb glucose for energy."

Causes:
   "Causes and Risk Factors:
Genetics: Family history of diabetes can increase risk.
Obesity: Excess body fat, especially around the abdomen, is a significant risk factor for type 2 diabetes.
Physical Inactivity: Lack of regular exercise contributes to weight gain and insulin resistance.
Poor Diet: Diets high in sugar, refined carbohydrates, and unhealthy fats can lead to weight gain and diabetes.
Age: Risk increases with age, especially after 45.
Gestational Diabetes: Having this condition during pregnancy raises the risk of developing type 2 diabetes later."

Symptoms:
"Symptoms of Diabetes:
Increased thirst and frequent urination
Extreme fatigue
Blurred vision
Slow healing of cuts and wounds
Unexplained weight loss (more common in type 1)
Increased hunger"

-Influenzas
   Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. It affects the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs, leading to a range of symptoms that can be mild to severe.

Causes:
"Causes of Influenza:

Transmission:

Person-to-Person Contact
Surface Contamination
Seasonal Changes
Crowded Environments
Compromised Immune System
Lack of Vaccination
Pre-existing Health Conditions
Travel:
Symptoms:
"Symptoms:
Common symptoms of influenza include:

Fever or chills
Cough
Sore throat
Runny or stuffy nose
Muscle or body aches
Headaches
Fatigue
Some people may experience vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children."

-Malaria
   "Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites of the Plasmodium species, which are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, South Asia, and South America."

Causes:
   Malaria is caused by protozoan parasites belonging to the Plasmodium genus, with Plasmodium falciparum being the most deadly strain. Other strains include Plasmodium vivax, Plasmodium ovale, and Plasmodium malariae."

Symptoms
   Symptoms typically appear 10 to 15 days after being bitten by an infected mosquito and can include:
Fever and chills
Sweats
Headaches
Nausea and vomiting
Muscle pain and fatigue
In severe cases, malaria can cause complications such as anemia, respiratory distress, and organ failure."

-Waterborne diseases
"Waterborne diseases are illnesses caused by pathogenic microorganisms that are transmitted through contaminated water. These diseases can occur when people consume, come into contact with, or use water that has been contaminated by fecal matter, chemical pollutants, or other harmful substances. Waterborne diseases can lead to significant health issues, particularly in areas with poor sanitation and inadequate access to clean drinking water."
"Cholera
Giardiasis
Dysentery
Hepatitis A
Typhoid Fever

Causes:
"Common Causes of Waterborne Diseases:
Bacteria:
Common bacterial pathogens include:
Escherichia coli (E. coli)
Salmonella
Vibrio cholerae (causes cholera)
Shigella
Viruses:
Viral pathogens often associated with waterborne diseases include:
Norovirus
Hepatitis A virus
Rotavirus
Parasites:
Waterborne parasites include:
Giardia lamblia (causes giardiasis)
Cryptosporidium (causes cryptosporidiosis)
Entamoeba histolytica (causes amoebic dysentery)"
Symptoms:
Diarrhea: Often watery, which can lead to dehydration.
Nausea and Vomiting: These can accompany diarrhea and can further lead to dehydration.
Abdominal Pain and Cramping: Discomfort in the stomach area.
Fever: Low-grade to high fever, depending on the infection.
Fatigue: General weakness or feeling tired.
Headaches: Common in many infections.
Muscle Pain: General aches and pains throughout the body.

-Food illness
Foodborne illness, commonly referred to as food poisoning, is caused by consuming contaminated food or beverages. These illnesses can result from bacteria, viruses, parasites, or chemical substances. Hereâ€™s an overview of key aspects related to foodborne illness

Causes:
"Common Causes of Foodborne Diseases
Bacteria:

Salmonella: Often found in raw or undercooked eggs, poultry, and meat.
Escherichia coli (E. coli): Found in undercooked beef, contaminated water, and unpasteurized milk and juices.
Listeria monocytogenes: Found in deli meats, hot dogs, and unpasteurized dairy products.
Campylobacter: Commonly found in raw or undercooked poultry.
Viruses:

Norovirus: A highly contagious virus often linked to contaminated food and water, particularly in crowded environments like cruise ships and restaurants."
Hepatitis A: Can be contracted from contaminated food, particularly shellfish or food prepared by an infected person.
Parasites:

Giardia: A parasite found in contaminated water and food, often affecting those who consume unclean water.
Toxoplasma: Found in undercooked meat, particularly pork and lamb.
Chemical Contaminants:

Pesticides: Residues from agricultural chemicals can contaminate fruits and vegetables.
Heavy Metals: Contaminants like lead, mercury, and cadmium can be present in some foods due to environmental pollution.

Symptoms:
Symptoms of foodborne illness can vary depending on the cause but often include:
Nausea
Vomiting
Diarrhea
Abdominal pain
Fever
Muscle aches
